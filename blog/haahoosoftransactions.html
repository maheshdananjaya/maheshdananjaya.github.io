Haahoos of Transactions- The Power of Two-phase Commit (2PC)

I am going to put down some thought here soon. Meantime check this presenation which dicusses the five forms of the two phase commit protocol. In this blog, I focus on synchronous network. But we can relate the dicusstion easily to asynchrnous or partially sycnrhnous networks. There are some established sentiments about 2PC.

Sentiment 1: 2PC is only for transactions with different objects and/or multiple operations.

Sentiment 2: 2PC is always blocking, thus we need 3PC.

In this blog post, I am going to convince you that these sentiments are slightly wrong and there are exceptions. 2PC is multi facets. For instance, 2PC can easily be used accomodate single-object read/write/RMWs over replicated key-value store (KVS). Such 2PC protocol is non-blocking and infact the basic building block of replicated databases and CAP theorem (check some early Phil Bernstein work as well).
